## A4 — Drawn Synthetic Fused Silica Tube Stock

**BoM position:** A4 — Intermediate in synthetic fused silica envelope fabrication (Stage A)
**Parent BoM item:** A5 — Cut & annealed tube blanks (trimmed from drawn tube stock for excimer lamp envelopes)
**Upstream input:** A3 — Vitrified synthetic fused silica ingot / cylinder (already investigated)
**Analysis date:** 2026-05-30

**Description.** Drawn synthetic fused silica tube stock is produced by reheating a vitrified ingot or cylinder (A3) in a vertical or horizontal tube-drawing lathe and pulling it continuously at controlled speed to achieve target outer diameter (OD) and wall thickness. For KrCl 222 nm excimer lamp envelopes, the relevant grade is Suprasil 310 or equivalent (~200 ppm OH), drawn to OD typically 15–64 mm with wall thicknesses of approximately 1–3 mm. The drawn tube is the first product in the Stage A chain that can be readily purchased as a standard catalogue item from fused silica suppliers (particularly Heraeus Covantics). It differs from the ingot in that tube drawing requires different capital equipment and expertise — not all ingot producers draw tubes, and not all tube drawers produce their own ingots.

---

### Confidence and completeness

Market reports for quartz tubing aggregate all grades (natural Type I/II quartz, semi-synthetic, and fully synthetic) without volume disaggregation by grade. The figures most frequently cited (~48,000 MT/year total quartz tube production; ~$585–630M market value in 2023–2024) cover all quartz tubing including low-cost natural quartz for the solar and general industrial markets. The sub-segment relevant here — fully synthetic, UV-grade, Suprasil-class tube stock — is a small, commercially sensitive fraction for which no producer publishes tonnage. Application breakdowns (semiconductor ~57%, UV lamp ~21%, photovoltaic ~15%) from one market research source carry opaque methodology and should be treated as indicative, not authoritative. Lamp dimensions from publicly listed products (QuantaLamp, WarOnFlu retailers) provide a basis for a bottom-up mass estimate. Overall data quality for the premium synthetic UV-grade sub-segment is low; the numbers below carry significant uncertainty.

### Producers and geographic concentration

**Heraeus Covantics (Germany)** is the dominant supplier of Suprasil-class synthetic fused silica lamp tubes for excimer applications globally. Their proprietary multi-step tube drawing process (referred to in technical literature; trade name not publicly confirmed) produces tubes in the 10–64 mm OD range from Suprasil 310 and related grades. Heraeus is fully vertically integrated from SiCl₄ precursor through ingot and tube. No production volume data published.

**Shin-Etsu Quartz Products Co., Ltd. (Japan)** — a joint venture between Shin-Etsu Chemical and Heraeus — produces SUPRASIL-F310 (~250 ppm OH) and F300 (<1 ppm OH) in tube and plate form. SQP uses a proprietary tool-free drawing method that avoids surface contact during forming, yielding clean inner and outer surfaces. SQP primarily serves the Japanese domestic market and Asian lamp manufacturers. Revenue and tonnage not disaggregated publicly.

**Momentive Performance Materials (US, formerly GE Quartz)** produces synthetic fused silica tubes for semiconductor and industrial applications; its position in the UV excimer lamp tube segment specifically is not confirmed.

**QSIL GmbH (Germany, acquired by SCHOTT AG in 2024)** produces fused silica tubes primarily for semiconductor and industrial applications; UV excimer lamp grade qualification is unconfirmed.

**Chinese producers (Feilihua, Dong-A Quartz, Pacific Quartz, Zhuoyue, others)** dominate global quartz tube production by volume but overwhelmingly serve natural-quartz applications (semiconductor process ware, solar, germicidal). Their qualification status for Suprasil-class 222 nm excimer lamp tube service is not confirmed in public sources.

Geographic concentration for Suprasil-class UV excimer lamp tube stock: the market is highly concentrated in Germany (Heraeus Covantics, Kleinostheim) and Japan (SQP), with no identified qualified producers in North America or China for this specific grade/application.

### Process notes specific to A4 (tube drawing step)

Several factors distinguish the A4 tube drawing step from a general commodity supply chain question:

- **Integration with A3:** At the premium lamp tube grade level, tube drawing is typically done by the same company that produces the ingot (Heraeus, SQP). The ingot is not widely traded as a feedstock to independent tube drawers at Suprasil quality levels. A3 and A4 are therefore operationally coupled for this grade.
- **Dimensional precision:** Lamp envelope OD and wall thickness tolerances affect seal quality, gas fill volume, and electrode fit. Tight OD tolerances (typically ±0.2–0.5 mm for lamp grades) require a controlled draw process with real-time diameter feedback. Higher-precision "HP" variants involve enhanced process control.
- **Surface cleanliness:** The inner surface of the tube is the primary boundary with the lamp fill gas. Surface contamination or hydroxyl groups from forming tools can affect lamp performance. Heraeus and SQP both emphasize tool-free or clean-process forming for UV lamp grades.
- **Drawing yield:** Not all drawn tube length is usable. End sections, diameter-transition zones, and sections with bubble/streak defects are culled. Yield from ingot to usable tube is unconfirmed but estimated at 70–85%. ❓
- **Tube length:** Drawn tube stock comes in lengths of typically 1–3 m. It is cut to lamp-body length at Stage A5, incurring further trim waste.

### Potential bottlenecks

- **Single-facility concentration for Suprasil-class excimer lamp tube stock.** Heraeus Covantics' Kleinostheim facility (Germany) appears to be the primary or sole qualified producer of Suprasil 310 tube stock for 222 nm excimer lamp applications globally. Any disruption to this facility — fire, flood, regulatory shutdown, labor action — would directly affect the ability of all 222 nm lamp manufacturers to source qualified envelope tubes.
- **Coupled ingot and tube supply.** Because the ingot-to-tube conversion is highly integrated at the premium grade level, a constraint on A3 ingot capacity propagates directly to A4 tube stock availability and vice versa. These two stages cannot easily be decoupled by sourcing from different suppliers.
- **Qualification time for new tube drawing sources.** A new supplier attempting to qualify Suprasil-class UV excimer lamp tube stock would need to demonstrate dimensional consistency, surface purity, radiation resistance at 222 nm, and operational lifetime in actual lamps. This process would likely take 2–5 years, given the specificity of requirements. No new entrant for this specific grade has been identified in public sources.
- **Capital equipment lead times.** High-precision tube-drawing lathes capable of sustained Suprasil-class production are specialty equipment. Procurement and commissioning of new drawing lines takes 1–3 years.
- **SQP's Heraeus JV structure.** Shin-Etsu Quartz Products is a joint venture with Heraeus. Its independence as an alternative supplier is limited by its corporate relationship with the same parent group. Whether SQP's tube drawing capacity is truly independent of Heraeus Covantics for surge capacity purposes is unclear.

---

### Alternative materials

No alternative material to synthetic fused silica (Suprasil 310 class) has been identified for KrCl 222 nm excimer lamp envelopes requiring high radiation resistance and long service life. As carried forward from A2 and A3 investigations: natural quartz fails radiation resistance requirements; sapphire cannot be tube-drawn at scale; CaF₂ is geometrically and mechanically unsuitable. The A4 step does not introduce any new substitution options.
