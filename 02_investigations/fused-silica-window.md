## D1 — Filter Substrate (UV-Grade Fused Silica Optical Window Blank)

**Parent BoM item:** D1 — Bandpass filter substrate for KrCl 222 nm far-UV lamp (Stage D)
**Date:** May 2026
**Status:** Research complete

**Description.** The Stage D bandpass filter for a KrCl 222 nm lamp consists of a flat optical window blank coated with a multilayer dielectric stack (HfO₂/SiO₂; covered separately in D2). The blank itself must be UV-grade synthetic fused silica (e.g. Heraeus Suprasil, Corning HPFS 7980, Chinese JGS1), which transmits to ~175 nm and has low intrinsic absorption at 222 nm. It is chemically identical in composition to the lamp envelope material (Stage A) but is procured in flat-polished window form rather than as tube stock. Typical geometry for a small KrCl lamp module is approximately 40–80 mm diameter, 1–3 mm thick. The primary commercial driver for UV-grade fused silica optical windows is semiconductor photolithography (193 nm ArF excimer), not UV disinfection. Natural fused quartz (electrically fused) is not a practical substitute at 222 nm due to higher OH variability and metallic impurities.

Data quality is moderate for the broader fused silica market but weak for the UV-grade optical window sub-segment specifically. The application-share breakdown is particularly uncertain: the frequently cited >35% semiconductor share appears to refer to all fused silica (including lower-grade process ware), not the UV-grade optical window sub-segment specifically. Consumption by far-UV lamps is not tracked by any source and is derived bottom-up here.

### Global annual production

- **Best guess:** ~700–1,500 t/year of UV-grade synthetic fused silica destined for precision optical components (windows, lenses, prisms, substrates) ❓
- **Range (low / high):** ~300 t / ~4,000 t
- **Reference year:** ~2023–2024
- **Method(s):**

**Method 1 — Market value divided by estimated unit price (window blanks):**
The UV fused silica window market was reported at ~$106M in 2022, projected ~$120M for 2023–2024 after applying the stated 5.5% CAGR. ⚠️

Representative pricing for UV fused silica optical window blanks (polished, precision grade, not AR-coated) ranges from ~$30 for a 25 mm diameter × 1 mm blank from Chinese suppliers to $200–600 for equivalent Corning/Heraeus-sourced blanks. A blend price of ~$80–150/piece for a "typical" traded optical window is assumed.

Estimated pieces/year = $120M ÷ $100/unit ≈ 1.2 million pieces. ❓

At ~50 mm diameter × 2 mm thick (a plausible filter-substrate geometry), each piece weighs approximately:
π × (2.5 cm)² × 0.2 cm × 2.2 g/cm³ ≈ **8.6 g**

But UV fused silica windows span a wide range of sizes. A simple-average weight of ~100 g/piece (spanning both small spectroscopy windows ~10 mm and large semiconductor optics ~200–300 mm) gives:

1.2M pieces × 0.1 kg/piece ≈ **120 tonnes** ⚠️

This is a lower bound; it excludes large-format wafers, boules sold for downstream fabrication, and bulk ingot sold to fab shops that then make multiple windows.

**Method 2 — Total fused silica market, optical-grade fraction:**
The total global fused silica market is ~$2.24B (2024), spanning bulk refractories and castables (~$0.8–1.2B, priced at ~$1–10/kg), semiconductor process ware (crucibles, tubes, rings; ~$0.5–0.8B), optical fiber preforms (huge volume, lower unit price), and precision optical components (small volume, high unit price).

A rough segmentation of the $2.24B market:
- Refractories / castables / powder: ~40% by value → ~$900M at ~$5/kg average → ~180,000 t
- Semiconductor process ware: ~25% by value → ~$560M at ~$50/kg average → ~11,200 t
- Optical fiber preforms: ~20% by value → ~$450M at ~$200/kg average → ~2,250 t
- Precision optical components (including UV windows): ~15% by value → ~$336M at ~$500–2,000/kg → **170–670 t** ❓

The UV window sub-market at ~$120M is roughly 36% of the optical components segment → implied ~60–240 t.

This cross-check suggests UV-grade optical windows specifically represent on the order of **100–500 t/year** of material as sold finished pieces. ❓

The two methods diverge: Method 1 yields a higher estimate if the average window is small and light; Method 2 gives a lower range. The divergence is large (100–500 t vs ~120 t piece-weight estimate) and reflects real uncertainty. A combined best-guess of **200–800 t/year** of UV-grade fused silica in optical window/substrate form is used, with wide uncertainty acknowledged.

Note: total synthetic fused silica production across all grades and forms (including fiber, semiconductor process ware) is much larger — likely >10,000 t/year from premium producers alone, and considerably more including lower-grade Chinese production. The UV optical window fraction is a small slice.

**Key assumptions:**
- "UV-grade fused silica window" means polished flat substrates (windows, filter blanks, wafers) transmitting to <200 nm, suitable for 222 nm applications
- Blend unit price of ~$100/piece used; actual range is very wide
- Optical components segment taken as ~15% of total fused silica market by value

### Producers and geographic concentration

The UV-grade synthetic fused silica optical window supply chain has three tiers. At the top, a small number of Western and Japanese producers supply the highest-purity ingot/boule material: **Heraeus** (Germany; Suprasil brand), **Corning** (USA; HPFS 7980), **Tosoh** (Japan), and **Shin-Etsu** (Japan; joint venture with Heraeus in China). These four are cited as the dominant producers of premium optical-grade synthetic fused silica globally; combined they reportedly account for ~42–50% of the high-purity synthetic fused silica market by revenue. ⚠️ Below them sits a large Chinese manufacturing base supplying JGS1/JGS2/JGS3 grade material (JGS1 ≈ UV grade, transmitting to ~190 nm), with dozens of optical fabricators operating in Jiangsu, Liaoning, and Guangdong provinces. China's CNBM/Lianyungang cluster produces over 20,000 t/year of fused silica across all grades, but the fraction meeting optical-UV specs is unknown. A third tier of precision optical fabricators (e.g. Edmund Optics, Knight Optical, Shanghai Optics, UQG Optics, Creator Optics) purchase ingot/blank from tier-1 producers and finish to specification. Geographic concentration in raw material production is therefore weighted toward Germany, Japan, and the US for the highest purity grades, with significant Chinese capacity for mid-grade material.

### Potential bottlenecks

- **Upstream SiCl₄ feedstock.** Synthetic fused silica is made from ultra-high-purity SiCl₄ (or OMCTS). SiCl₄ is itself a constrained specialty chemical (analysed separately in A1). Any tightness in SiCl₄ supply propagates to fused silica ingot.
- **Ingot-pulling capacity and capital intensity.** High-OH UV-grade boules require dedicated furnaces with tightly controlled flame-hydrolysis or VAD deposition equipment. Lead times to add capacity are multi-year. The Heraeus/Shin-Etsu Shenyang JV (¥500M investment, ~2024 startup) illustrates the scale of capital required.
- **Precision polishing and finishing throughput.** Filter blanks require double-side lapping and polishing to λ/10 or better surface flatness; this is a skilled, equipment-intensive step. At volumes of millions of units, optical fabrication capacity could become relevant even if raw ingot supply is adequate.
- **Grade qualification for 222 nm service.** Not all UV-grade fused silica performs equally under sustained 222 nm irradiation; solarisation resistance varies by OH content and metallic impurity level. Only a subset of available grades (high-OH, low-metals) are suitable for long-life excimer service, narrowing the practical supplier base.
- **Chinese supply chain for JGS1:** The large Chinese optical fabrication base could supply filter substrates at scale and low cost, but quality consistency (particularly for sustained UV exposure) may require independent qualification. Trade restrictions or export controls could also affect access to Chinese fused silica stocks.

### Alternative materials

Synthetic fused silica is the standard filter substrate at 222 nm because of its deep-UV transmission, dimensional stability, and low thermal expansion. Alternatives with some UV-C capability include:

- **Natural fused quartz (electrically fused):** Lower cost, widely available, but higher metallic impurities and OH variability; solarisation under sustained 222 nm irradiation is a concern. May be acceptable for short-life or low-intensity applications.
- **MgF₂ (magnesium fluoride):** Transmits to ~115 nm, highly UV-stable; used in VUV optics. More expensive, more brittle, harder to polish flat; niche use in demanding UV applications.
- **CaF₂ (calcium fluoride):** Transmits to ~125 nm; used in deep-UV lithography as a substitute for fused silica at 193 nm. Significantly more expensive than fused silica; primary supply is concentrated in Russia and China.
- **UV-transmitting borosilicate or specialty glass:** Some grades (e.g. Schott Borofloat) transmit to ~280–300 nm, not to 222 nm. Not a substitute for far-UV filter substrates.

For most far-UV lamp applications, synthetic fused silica is the practical choice; no drop-in substitute offers the same combination of cost, availability, UV transmission, and mechanical workability.
